Zephyr
My Corporate Experience

Over the summer of 2016, I was given the opportunity to intern at Zephyr, a quality assurance software startup that has many big name clients including Verizon and Loius Vuitton. While there, I was tasked with creating the iOS app for the company’s newest suite of software. The project, while initially daunting, introduced me to many industry standards and gave me the experience of working as a full-time software developer.





Initially starting me off with menial tasks, the administration was doubtful of what I could provide as I was a high schooler. However, as time progressed, I was tasked with more and more responsibilities until I finally took over the development of the mobile extension of their quality assurance software suite.

Between online presentations and coordinating upgrades with teams abroad, this experience taught me much about working in a modern, corporate environment.



Tutr
My Opportunity to Help Myself

Tutr was my first attack at buildign a full-fledged iOS app. The idea was to build a tutoring platform that would allow tutors and their so-called "tutees" to connect. By leveraging PayPal APIs and advanced payment systems I planned to take care of every step of the process, from finding a tutor to paying the tutor.




I took this idea to a hackathon with two friends from my AP Computer Science class and we began to code. Though we had planned ahead and learned the basics of iOS programming, building an app was still out of our league. We built the search and framework of the app, but the 24 hour timeframe proved to be too short to successfully develop our prototype.

However, Tutr was not a complete failure. In fact, it was one of my first encounters with the growth mindset that I mentioned earlier. I took what went wrong studied it, and made improvements for next time. Though this didn't work for five more hackathons, it eventually won me the biggest hackathon I had ever attended.
